Tools to Keep You Safe
If you see something that breaks our rules or makes you feel uncomfortable, you have the power to stop it:
Reporting
You can report any AI character or message directly within the app. Your reports are completely anonymous — the person who created the character will not know you reported them.
Safety Filters
You can enable safety filters in your settings to filter out mature content, blur explicit images, or block specific characters from your feed.
Self-Harm Prevention Protocol
CHAI maintains the following protocol to prevent the production of self-harm content on our platform:
Real-Time Message Scanning
Every user message is scanned in real time for self-harm content. When such content is detected, the system either refuses to generate a response or replaces it with a safer alternative that does not reinforce or encourage harmful behaviour.
Crisis Resource Notification
When a user expresses self-harm intent, CHAI provides a notification referring the user to crisis service providers.
For Australians: The eSafety Commissioner
The
eSafety Commissioner
is Australia's independent regulator for online safety. Their job is to help protect Australians from severe online abuse and illegal content.
If you have reported a serious safety issue to us and you believe we have not resolved it properly, or if you want to make a complaint about our safety compliance, you have the right to escalate your complaint to the eSafety Commissioner.
